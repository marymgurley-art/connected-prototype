# Connected — Product Vision

## What This Product Does
Connected is a real-time family communication app for memory care facilities, inspired by children's daycare apps. It keeps families connected throughout the day — not just at the end — with live updates, photos, menus, and social calendars. Families can also send pictures, videos, and voice messages back to their loved one, and build a personal profile that helps staff connect with the resident as a whole person — not just a patient.

## The Problem We're Solving
Families with a loved one in memory care often feel helpless, guilty, and disconnected. They can't visit as often as they'd like. They don't know what their loved one's day looks like. They feel like they're missing it. Connected bridges that gap — giving families a window into their loved one's daily life and a way to stay present even when they can't be there in person.

## Who It's For

### Primary User: The Family Member
Adult children, spouses, and relatives of memory care residents. They have full lives — jobs, kids, distance — that make frequent visits hard. They carry guilt about that. The emotional job of this app is to reduce that guilt by helping them feel genuinely connected to their loved one's daily life.

**Prototype persona: Sarah**
- Daughter of Judy, a resident in her early 80s
- Lives nearby but has a full-time job and young children
- Hasn't visited in two weeks
- Checks her phone between meetings
- Wants to feel like she knows what her mom's day looks like

### Secondary User: Care Staff
Resident Assistants (RAs) and care staff who log and share updates throughout the day. The app should make their documentation feel meaningful — not like paperwork, but like connection. Staff also receive and deliver messages from families to residents at appropriate moments in the day.

### The Resident
Memory care residents themselves are not direct app users, but they are at the center of everything. The app should treat them as full human beings with histories, personalities, preferences, and lives — not as patients to be monitored.

**Prototype persona: Judy**
- Early 80s, memory care resident, Room 412, 4th Floor
- Knows who her family members are
- Has difficulty forming coherent sentences, maintaining conversation, following directions
- Experiences paranoia and some depression
- Before her diagnosis: loved watercolor painting, golf, playing cards on Thursday nights, following the stock market, Patsy Cline
- Responds well to one-on-one conversation, eye contact, and hearing her name
- Loves looking at family photos
- Can feel anxious in loud or unfamiliar situations

## Core Features

### For Family Members
- **Live Feed** — a real-time timeline of the resident's day: meals, activities, staff notes, photos. Updated throughout the day, not just at the end.
- **Daily Calendar** — the facility's activity schedule for the day, so families know what their loved one is doing and when. Activities the resident actually attended are marked.
- **Resident Profile** — a family-filled profile documenting the resident's life story, loves, preferences, and helpful notes for staff. Feels like a scrapbook, not a medical form.
- **Send a Moment** — families can send photos, short videos, and voice messages to their loved one. Staff receive these and share them with the resident at a good moment in their day.
- **Announcements & Messaging** — facility-wide announcements and direct messaging between families and staff.
- **Resources Page** — curated support for families navigating the grief and loss of a loved one with dementia, Alzheimer's, or other memory diseases. Articles, helplines, caregiver support groups.

### For Care Staff
- **Update Feed** — a simple interface for posting updates, photos, and meal notes throughout the day
- **Incoming Messages** — a queue of photos, videos, and voice messages sent by families, with guidance on when and how to share them with residents
- **Resident Profiles** — access to family-filled profiles to help staff connect with residents as individuals

## The Emotional Design Principle
This app should feel like a warm hug, not a monitoring dashboard. Every design decision — color, typography, copy, interaction — should reinforce that the resident is a loved person with a full life, and that the family member is doing the best they can.

The watercolor painting moment is the north star: a family member seeing their loved one engaged in something they've always loved, even now, is the feeling this whole product is built around.

---

## Design Direction
- **Palette:** Warm whites, soft peach, sage green, warm gray. Nothing clinical or cold.
- **Typography:** Friendly but not childish. Clean and readable.
- **Mobile-first:** Designed for any phone. No app store required.
- **Tone:** Warm, human, simple. Staff-facing copy should be encouraging, not bureaucratic.

## What This Product Is NOT
- Not a medical records system
- Not a clinical data or health information platform
- Not a replacement for care staff — it supplements and enhances their communication with families
- Not a surveillance or monitoring tool
- Not just for dementia — designed for any memory care resident and their family

---

## How It's Built
Evaluating no-code and low-code tools that support real-time updates, photo sharing, two-way messaging, and simple data entry for care staff. Current prototype is HTML/CSS/JS built with Claude Code.

## Status
Early concept and planning phase. First prototype in progress, focused on the family member experience.
