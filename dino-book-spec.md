# Interactive Dinosaur Book — Product Spec

## Overview
A web-based interactive dinosaur book for kids age 6. Each page features a lesser-known dinosaur with a dramatic photo, a fun paragraph, tap-to-reveal facts, sound effects, and a mini-game. Built with a no-code tool. No logins. No scary content. Pure discovery and delight.

## The 15 Dinosaurs

### 1. Amargasaurus
- **Photo/Illustration:** Side profile showing neck spines dramatically
- **Paragraph:** "Amargasaurus had the wildest hairdo in dinosaur history — two rows of spiky spines running all the way down its neck! Nobody is totally sure what those spines were for. Maybe they made it look bigger to scare away enemies!"
- **Tap-to-reveal facts:**
  - It was as long as a school bus!
  - It lived in South America 130 million years ago
  - Its name means "La Amarga lizard" — named after the place it was found
- **Sound:** Low rumbling stomp
- **Mini-game:** Matching — match the dino to its body part

## Mini-Game Types (Full Definitions)

### 🦴 Fossil Dig
- Screen shows a patch of dirt
- Child taps to brush away dirt layer by layer
- Bones appear one at a time
- When all bones are revealed, the full dinosaur appears with a celebratory sound

### 🥚 Egg Hatch
- A big egg sits in the center
- Child taps it — cracks appear with each tap
- After 5 taps the egg bursts open and a baby dino pops out
- Confetti and happy sounds

### 🔀 Matching Game
- 4 cards face down on each side
- Flip to match pairs (dino + food, dino + sound, dino + home)
- Correct match: cards light up and chime
- Wrong match: cards flip back with a gentle buzz

### ❓ Quiz
- One question, three picture answers
- Tap your answer
- Correct: big cheer, star animation
- Wrong: gentle "try again" and the answer highlights

## No-Code Tool Requirements

| Requirement | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Image embedding | Full-page dino photos |
| Text formatting | Big headers, readable paragraphs |
| Click/tap interactions | Egg hatch, fact reveals |
| Sound embedding | Roars, stomps, sound effects |
| Mini-game embedding | Via iframe (e.g. Genially, BookWidgets) |
| Works in browser | Desktop and tablet |
| No login required | Child uses it independently |

**Recommended tools to evaluate:**
- Webflow (layout + interactions)
- Glide (simple, tablet-friendly)
- Genially (great for interactive content + games built-in)
- Notion (simple but limited on interactions)

**Top recommendation: Genially** — it has built-in interactivity, sound support, and game templates, all without coding.

## Content Rules
- Age 6 reading level: short sentences, common words
- Tone: excited, warm, encouraging — lots of "!"
- No blood, hunting gore, or scary predator framing
- Facts should feel surprising and delightful, not like homework
- Every page ends with something to DO (game, sound, reveal)
